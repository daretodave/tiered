§ 01 · Palette

Warm ink on warm paper.

Never pure black; never pure white. Paper carries a deliberate brown undertone so the architecture feels carved from stone, not screen. One ceremonial gold is the only saturated color in the chrome — everything else stays close to the neutral spine.

§ 02 · Sentiment

Six muted signals.

Rank movement is editorial information, not a casino. Each sentiment is a single low-saturation hue, used sparingly and always paired with text — never as a standalone color cue. AA-tested against paper and ink at 14px.

§ 03 · Type

Serif for voice. Sans for tools. Mono for ranks.

Editorial weight rests on Source Serif 4 — a contemporary text serif with a generous italic. Inter handles the interface chrome. JetBrains Mono carries every number, rank, and code-adjacent label so quantities never compete with prose.

§ 04 · Spacing

An 8-pixel skeleton.

All gaps, padding, and rhythm derive from a single 8px unit, with one 4px half-step for tight typographic adjustments. Use 24 / 32 / 48 for editorial breathing room; 64 / 96 for chapter breaks.

§ 05 · Surfaces

Three radii. Three opacities.

The shadow contract is the strictest rule in the system: a single 1px y-offset, no blur, no spread. Depth is implied by line and tone, never by haze. Three radii cover everything from chips to hero cards.

§ 06 · Motion

Three durations. Four curves.

Motion is editorial punctuation, not entertainment. Quick (120ms) for direct response, base (240ms) for state change, calm (480ms) for entrances and ranking shifts. prefers-reduced-motion collapses every transform to a plain opacity fade.
