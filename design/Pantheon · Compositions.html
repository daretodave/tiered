§ 01 · Home / "/"

The cold-search landing.

Most readers arrive here from a search like "ranked survivor seasons no spoilers." The first 3 seconds must communicate (a) which site they're on, (b) that it's spoiler-safe, (c) that there's a ranking here. Everything below is patience.

01 · Eye hits first

Survivor cover block

The featured show takes the left half of the hero — its paper color, its name set huge in show-ink serif, its blurb in italic. No illustration. The color and the type do the work.

02 · Eye hits second

The promise

"The seasons, ranked. no spoilers." Serif headline, italic on the promise, primary gold on the only word that matters.

03 · No-spoilers shield

Implicit + explicit

Implicit in the tagline. Explicit as a pill badge on every show-home and season page. Never a popup, never a modal.

04 · Vote affordance

Not on home

The home page never solicits a vote. Voting only appears once a reader is on a specific season page. Home is for orientation.

§ 02 · Show home / "/[show]"

The show, on its own terms.

Every show takes over its own page chrome. Background, ink, accent — all switch to the show's three-color palette. The show name appears at 112px serif on a wall of show-paper; the right column carries the tagline, lede, and shield. No artwork — the color and the wordmark are the identity.

01 · Eye hits first

The show's name in show-ink at 112px on the show's paper. Huge, calm, editorial. The way a magazine puts the title on a cover.

02 · Eye hits second

§ 03 · Season / "/[show]/season/[n]"

The page a reader actually came for.

A single column of editorial — a colored breadcrumb bullet, season title, both rankings, a 200-word blurb, and the vote pair. The comment thread runs alongside on desktop, beneath on mobile. The shield is inline, no longer a pill — by this depth the reader has earned the trust.

§ 04 · Interactions

Three things a reader can actually do.

These are live. Click them. The components below are the production code — same React, same tokens, same easings. Each one carries a reduced-motion contract baked in.

Vote pair
primary

Click +/−: count animates by 1, button flashes sentiment color (warm-up / warm-down) for 800ms, locks 800ms to prevent fat-finger doubles. Reduced-motion: count number simply fades; no flash, no slide.

Comment input
primary

Focus: stub expands into textarea with the no-spoilers reminder. Type "wins" or "final tribal": red border, inline flag with the exact phrase, post button locks. Editorial review still happens server-side; AI flag is a first filter, not the gate.

Rank shift pill
passive

72-hour pill: appears on a season card after a canon update. Sentiment-colored (↑warm-up, ↓warm-down, —neutral, ◆hold). Pill auto-dismisses; the new rank position becomes the new normal.

Knowledgeable peer. Confident, warm, plain-spoken, never pretentious. The reader is a friend who watches everything and wants the truth without spoilers. No exclamation points unless quoted.
