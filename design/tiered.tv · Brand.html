Brand · the mark, the header, the footer

One mark.
Every page.

The tiered.tv brand mark is three horizontal bars, stacked, descending in width. It is the only SVG illustration in the entire product. It appears in every header and every footer, in one of three modes — dark default, light secondary, and tinted on a show's paper. It is shared. It is the system's single illustration. Do not invent variants.

§ 01 · The mark

Three bars, descending.

The mark fits a 28-unit square viewBox. Three horizontal bars stacked vertically — widths 20, 14, 8 — say what the brand says: a hierarchy, rendered as type. Each bar's left edge is flush at x=4. Row gaps are 3 units; bar height is 4. Every value is an integer. currentColor fill, no stroke.

§ 02 · Size scale

From favicon to about-page hero.

The same SVG, rendered at six standard sizes. Stroke scales with the glyph — the integer viewBox means crisp edges at every step. Never render below 16px.

§ 03 · Color modes

Two defaults. Three tints.

The mark always uses currentColor, so it picks up the surrounding ink token automatically. Dark on a tiered.tv paper, dark-ink on a light paper, show-ink on a show paper. Three behaviors, one file.

§ 06 · Do · don't

Four rules for keeping the mark itself.

The mark is small, geometric, and built on integers — which means almost any modification breaks it. Treat it as a finished object, not a starting point.

Do

Render at the six published sizes.

16 / 22 / 28 / 48 / 96 / 240. These are the only sizes the mark is allowed to appear at. Pixel-snap any new placement.

Don't

Never recolor with a gradient.

The mark uses currentColor only. Editorial gold, ink, show-ink — fine. Gradients, glows, drop-shadows, outlines, fills — never.

Don't

Never invent per-show variants.

No "Survivor tiered.tv mark with a flame on top." No "Drag Race mark with sequins." The mark is universal — the show's identity comes from color and wordmark, never from a custom glyph.

Don't

Never crop or rotate.

All three bars must stay horizontal and left-aligned at x=4. The descending widths — 20, 14, 8 — are the mark; do not equalize them, reverse them, or tighten the gaps. Crop the bounding box if you must — never the bars themselves.

§ 07 · For the autonomous loop

The mark is finished.

If a future loop is tempted to redesign the brand mark, add per-show variants, or replace the wordmark — read this section and stop.

The tiered.tv brand mark is the only SVG illustration in the entire product. Its source is the inline <svg viewBox="0 0 28 28"> block found in every page header. It is three horizontal bars on integer coordinates, filled in currentColor, with no stroke anywhere. It is shared across every show.

The wordmark "tiered.tv" is set in Source Serif 4, weight 600, all lowercase, with the mark sitting 10px to its left. This lockup appears in every header and footer. It does not change. There are no per-show wordmark variants.

Where you would otherwise be tempted to add a custom show glyph, mascot, badge, or icon — use the bullet pattern: a filled circle in var(--show-primary) at 8–16px. See tiered.tv · Show Identity.html § 03 for the canonical examples.

Files that carry the mark: tiered.tv · Tokens.html, tiered.tv · Show Identity.html, tiered.tv · Compositions.html, tiered.tv · Brand.html, tiered.tv · Survivor.html, and every production page via compositions/screens.jsx (<TopNav>). Change in one place — the inline SVG — only with explicit human review.
