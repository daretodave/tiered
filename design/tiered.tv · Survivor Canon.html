01 · WHO

One editor, named.

Each show gets a single canon editor. For Survivor, that's M. Reyes, who has rewatched every season at least twice in the last three years. The canon is one opinion, defended in public.

02 · HOW

Position, not score.

Seasons aren't given numbers out of ten. They're placed against each other, one slot at a time. If two seasons argue for the same slot, we write the argument and choose. There are no ties.

03 · WHEN

Revised quarterly.

The canon moves slowly. Quarterly revisions, with public diff notes. The community rank, on the other tab, updates every Thursday — that's where the noise lives.

If a friend asks where to start with Survivor and you've got one answer, it comes from this drawer. Reading from one is a complete argument for the genre.

01 — 05 · five entries
01

Heroes vs. Villains

Season 20 · 2010 · Samoa

The format at its loudest, finally with a cast that had earned the volume.

A returnees season that finally let the format show what it could really do, when nobody had to be introduced. The first day plays like the eighth day of any other season. We'd defend it without footnotes — twenty-five years in, this is the season Survivor will still be measured against.

02

Borneo

Season 1 · 2000 · Pulau Tiga

The genre, invented mid-air.

The first season is also one of the best, which is unfair, and it's the kind of unfair the show has never recovered from. You can see them deciding what reality television is going to mean while they're shooting it. The cast is too smart for the show to lie to them; the editing learns its grammar in real time.

03

Pearl Islands

Season 7 · 2003 · Panama

Pirates, marooning, theater — a season that knew what it was.

The marooning premise is theatre, and the show commits to the bit. Production trusts the cast to be the show, and the cast obliges. The mid-season twist is the rare one that earned its setup; everything after it, including the parts you'd rather not relitigate, is the cost of doing business when the show is this confident.

Community↑ 1
#03
Why this slot
The clearest example of premise meeting cast in the show's history.
04

Micronesia

Season 16 · 2008 · Palau

The meta-season that earned its meta.

Fans vs. Favorites was a premise the show should have been allergic to and instead pulled off with a kind of weary mastery. The "favorites" know the show is a show and play that way; the "fans" don't, and the friction between those two registers carries the whole season. The final third is some of the show's most patient editing.

05

Cambodia

Season 31 · 2015 · Koh Rong Samloem

An all-returnee gamble that, against the odds, worked.

The show had been doing returnees for fifteen years and finally figured out what they were for. The cast was selected for argument, not nostalgia, and the resulting season is the show at its most legible — every move you watch is doing two or three things at once. The pace doesn't slow.
