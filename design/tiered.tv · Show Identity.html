Phase 02 · Show identity · v0.2 · supersedes the facade direction

Color and typography.
Nothing else.

A show's identity is its three colors and its name set in serif. No facades, no sigils, no per-show illustration. The color does the visual work. The serif does the editorial work. Everything else is text.

§ 01 · The rule

One rule. Three corollaries.

tiered.tv's first visual prototype generated per-show SVG art — torches, sequins, spoons, friezes. It read as AI-generated and did not meet the bar. The system has been re-grounded: every show is identified by color and type alone.

Do · the rule

A show is its palette and its wordmark.

Three colors (paper, ink, primary) and a name set in Source Serif 4. Render the name at 80–112px on its paper, in its ink, and the identity is complete. No illustration needed.

Don't · ever

Never generate per-show SVG illustration.

No facades. No sigils. No mascots. No torches, sequins, spoons, herbs, columns, pediments, friezes, or any other thematic ornamentation. This direction was tried and rejected. Do not retry, do not "improve," do not add "just one small icon."

Do · for list markers

Use a colored bullet, in the show's primary.

Where a list needs a visual signal of which show owns a row, place a 12–16px filled circle in var(--show-primary). This is the only show-specific graphic in the system. Class name: .bullet.

Do · brand mark only

One SVG, shared across the whole product.

The tiered.tv mark — three horizontal bars, stacked, descending in width — appears in the topbar of every page. It is the only SVG illustration in the system. Do not invent per-show variants of it.

§ 02 · The shows

Three shows. Three rooms.

Each show takes over the chrome with its three-color palette. The wordmark, in show-ink at 96–112px on show-paper, is the hero. The right column carries the data — palette, season count, blurb, tagline — and a single bullet, to show what the marker pattern looks like in context.

§ 03 · The bullet, in context

The list pattern.

When a list crosses shows — themed lists, search results, comment threads at scale — each row carries the show's primary as a 12px bullet. The reader can scan a column of bullets and know what's where, without a single icon being drawn.

The 12 most reviled finales · cross-show ↓ 12 entries

Why a bullet is enough.

A reader recognizes color faster than they recognize illustration. Coral means Survivor. Copper means Top Chef. Pink means Drag Race. Three weeks in, the bullet is a wordless caption — and it costs nothing to draw.

This pattern replaces every per-show icon, sigil, badge, and avatar slot the original prototype reached for.

  • Size: 12px in dense lists, 14–16px in cards, 10px in breadcrumbs.
  • Color: always var(--show-primary). Never tinted, never gradient.
  • Alignment: baseline-centered to the row's primary text size.
  • Pair with: the show name in text, never alone. The bullet is a marker, not an identifier.
§ 04 · Chrome tinting

The same shell. Three different rooms.

When a reader enters a show, the page tokens flip from neutral chrome to the show's palette. The components do not change. The chrome does not redraw. The color and the wordmark are doing all the work — exactly as designed.

tiered.tv is an editorial reality-TV ranking site whose visual identity rests on color and typography. The system was originally prototyped with per-show SVG illustration (facades, sigils, ornamental friezes); that direction reads as AI-generated and was rejected in May 2026. Do not retry it.

  1. NEVER generate SVG illustration for individual shows. No facades, sigils, mascots, ornaments. Do not "improve" the rejected direction. Do not add "just one small icon."
  2. Where a show needs a list marker, use a 12–16px filled circle in var(--show-primary). Class: .bullet. This is the only show-specific graphic permitted.
  3. The only SVG in the product is the tiered.tv brand mark — three horizontal bars descending in width (20 / 14 / 8 on a 28-unit viewBox), monochromatic, shared across every page. Located in the topbar. Do not invent per-show variants.
  4. Color identity flows through chrome tinting. When a reader is on a show page, the chrome tokens (--show-paper, --show-ink, --show-primary) override the neutral defaults. Big colored blocks holding huge serif type ARE the hero treatment.
  5. Type does the heavy lifting. Source Serif 4 for editorial (40–112px on heroes). Inter for chrome. JetBrains Mono for ranks. When in doubt, make the type bigger before reaching for ornament.
  6. Show identity is five fields and no more: name, paper, ink, primary, seasons, plus a blurb and tagline string. If a future contributor proposes a sixth field that is graphical in nature, reject it.
