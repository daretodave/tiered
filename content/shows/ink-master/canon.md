# Editor's Canon — Ink Master

## 1. Season One

tag: The founding season — the format's first real test of a working artist's skill under a ticking clock.
slot_argument: Ink Master started with a credibility problem to prove — real artists, real clients, real ink. It earns the top slot by default, but also because the technical judging is already legible here before the franchise multiplied into spinoffs.
community_rank_hint: rank=1 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season One is the only entry so far, and it's the clear call at #1. Ink Master's format arrives close to fully formed here: working artists, live elimination challenges, real clients who don't get do-overs, and a judging panel pulled from the tattoo industry rather than reality TV. Dave Navarro hosts a premise built on real stakes — a bad line stays on someone's skin. The show's later spinoffs would multiply the format into team battles and grudge rematches, but this debut is where the credibility gets established: technical judging, working artists, and permanent consequences, before any of the franchise's later flourishes.
