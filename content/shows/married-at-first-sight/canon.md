# Editor's Canon — Married at First Sight

## 1. New York

tag: The founding season that proved strangers could commit to marriage on camera before ever meeting.
slot_argument: Experts match strangers who meet for the first time at the altar and marry immediately, no precedent to lean on. New York sets the rules the franchise still runs by — blind match, legal vows, a trial period before anyone can walk away.
community_rank_hint: rank=1 delta=0 sentiment=hold

New York holds the top slot not because it's flashy, but because it set the standard every later season has to clear: does the experiment still feel like a real risk, or has the format calmed into routine? This canon rewards seasons where the stakes feel unresolved going in — where the couples seem genuinely unsure, not performing uncertainty for the cameras. As eighteen more seasons get seeded here, the ones that climb will be the ones that recapture that original uncertainty rather than lean on the franchise's now-familiar rhythm. The ones that slide will be the ones playing it safe. New York sets the bar because it never had a script to fall back on.
