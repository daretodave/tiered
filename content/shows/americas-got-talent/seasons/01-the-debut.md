Regis Philbin anchors the first America's Got Talent as a broadcaster with no competing template — the show is genuinely uncertain what it will find in its audition rooms. Piers Morgan, Sharon Osbourne, and David Hasselhoff form a judging panel with distinct, clashing instincts. The open-call promise runs widest here: singers, comedians, acrobats, novelty acts, and performers the format had no category for all share the same stage. The debut roughness is inseparable from its energy.
