The St. Lucia season is structurally unlike any other Below Deck run. A mid-season captain change — Lee Rosbach commanding the first three episodes and the final four, with Sandy Yawn bridging the bulk — gave the season a shape neither captain fully owns. The Piton-shadowed Caribbean waters offered a lush backdrop. Crew dynamics adjusted around the transition, adding a layer of uncertainty the format doesn't usually carry. Serviceable, but the structural seam shows.
