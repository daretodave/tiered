Thailand brought Below Deck to Southeast Asia for the first time, anchoring in the Andaman Sea off Phuket with its distinctive limestone islands and aquamarine bays. The location gave the season a visual texture no Caribbean run could replicate, and the twenty-episode order allowed the crew dynamics to develop across a longer arc than most seasons. Charter guests navigated an unfamiliar region, sharpening the service pressure in distinct ways. Captain Lee held command across the franchise's longest season.
