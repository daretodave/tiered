# Editor's Canon — The Real Housewives of Dubai

## 1. Season 1

tag: The founding season that gave the franchise its Gulf-set international wealth story.
slot_argument: Six women enter Dubai's expat elite — a wealth-and-status world built on international money rather than old-versus-new local money. The cast is confident for a debut, and the season sets the terms for everything RHODubai becomes.
community_rank_hint: rank=1 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season one earns its spot because it's the version of RHODubai every later season gets checked against: does the international expat wealth-and-status world still generate real tension, or has the show settled into imitating the franchise's older beats? This canon rewards seasons where the friction feels native to the cast and the specific pressures of an expat social scene, not manufactured for a plot twist. As a second season gets seeded here, whichever one rises will be the one that pushes the show's central wealth-versus-status dynamic somewhere new rather than replay it. The one that falls will be coasting on personality alone. Season one set the bar because nothing about it felt secondhand.
