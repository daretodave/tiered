Five women, one city, no precedent for what an Atlanta Housewives season could be. The debut cast — each with a distinct relationship to Atlanta's social hierarchy and a different idea of what the cameras were there to document — arrived with more than enough to fill a first season. The show was still discovering its format, which gives the season an unpolished texture. That roughness is part of what makes it worth watching: the foundation laid in plain sight.
