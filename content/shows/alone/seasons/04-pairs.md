Season four swapped the show's defining premise — radical solitude — for something more complex: seven pairs of survivalists dropped in Quatsino Sound, tasked with surviving together. The isolation that made the first three seasons compelling is shared here, and sharing it produces a different kind of tension. Individual survival skills still matter, but the relational layer introduces variables the format wasn't designed around. An essential season for what it teaches about why the original works.
