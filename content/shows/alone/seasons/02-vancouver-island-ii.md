The second season returned to Quatsino Sound on a simple test: was the format a novelty or something sturdier? The answer was clear. Ten new survivalists arrived on Vancouver Island better prepared than their predecessors — they had watched season one and thought harder about gear choices and shelter strategy. The production rhythm was more assured, the self-filmed footage better calibrated. A return run that earned its repeat rather than trading on the origin's novelty.
