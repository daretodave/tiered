Sixteen contestants split into two teams, set to work on a business task each week, then
brought into Trump's boardroom to account for what happened. The format invented itself
on NBC in January 2004 and the cast played it straight — no celebrity leverage, no
self-referential posturing. The tasks were grounded, the boardroom had weight, and the
show's grammar was built cleanly. Every season after runs inside the vocabulary this
