# Editor's Canon — Too Hot to Handle

## 1. Season 1 (2020)

tag: The founding season that built the no-contact format from scratch.
slot_argument: A boatload of serial daters, one AI enforcer, and a shared cash prize that shrinks with every broken rule. The debut cast plays it straight and plays it for the cameras in equal measure, and the show never blinks at either choice.
community_rank_hint: rank=1 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season one is the only entry ranked so far, and it takes the top slot by default, it's the blueprint every later season builds on. The premise arrives fully formed: a villa full of serial daters, an AI host named Lana laying down one hard rule, and a shared prize pool that drops every time someone breaks it. The debut cast leans all the way into the format's tension between genuine connection and pure performance, and the show never punishes that ambiguity, it plays it as the whole point. There's a rawness here that later, more polished seasons will trade away for scale.
