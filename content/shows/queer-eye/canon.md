# Editor's Canon — Queer Eye

## 1. Georgia

tag: The founding season — Netflix's reboot introduces its format and its ensemble with total confidence.
slot_argument: Season one sets the format's whole grammar in eight episodes: five specialists, one nominee, one home, one week. The Fab Five's chemistry is instant, and Georgia gives the show a grounded backdrop.
community_rank_hint: { rank: 1, delta: 0, sentiment: hold }

Season one earns the top slot by default and by merit — it's the only entry so far, but it also happens to nail the format on the first try. Atlanta and its Georgia suburbs give the show a specific place to work from, and the Fab Five's five distinct lanes — food, fashion, culture, design, grooming — combine into something that feels like a genuine ensemble rather than five separate segments stitched together. The show never overexplains its own premise; it just gets to work. Eight episodes is enough to establish a rhythm without repeating itself. Everything the franchise becomes starts here.
