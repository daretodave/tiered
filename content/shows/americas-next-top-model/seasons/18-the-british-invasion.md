Cycle 18's split-cast format is the most structurally interesting experiment the late
CW era attempted. Pitting nine British contestants against nine Americans generates
a competitive dynamic that reads differently from the standard open casting — national
loyalties add a layer the competition can actually use. London's fashion industry
backdrop gives the early episodes strong editorial context, and the Hawaii finale
stretch delivers a visual contrast. The format works better than most of the cycle's
contemporaries managed.
