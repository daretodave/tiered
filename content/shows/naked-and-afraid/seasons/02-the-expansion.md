Season two confirmed the format wasn't a novelty. Discovery brought a more deliberate casting approach and sent new pairings to locations that pushed the 21-day structure into different terrain. The pairing dynamic, still finding its shape in season one, became the show's primary editorial lever: two strangers, no reference point, no clothing, genuine environmental pressure. The season that answered whether the format had a future worth pursuing.
