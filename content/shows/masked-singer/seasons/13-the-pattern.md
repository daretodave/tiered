Season thirteen is The Masked Singer at its most pattern-settled. Thirteen cycles of the same disguise conceit, the same panel ensemble, the same Fox production machinery — and the show still finding an audience that comes back for the ritual of it. The costume design held at the level the established era demands. The celebrity roster delivered enough genuine guessing work to justify the format. The show's identity at this point is the consistency itself: no reinvention required, none offered.
