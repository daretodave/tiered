Season twenty-eight ran eleven episodes with a competitive celebrity cast and the easy, confident Bergeron-Andrews rhythm that had defined the format since season two. Nothing announced itself as a final chapter — it simply ran well and then ended. Only in hindsight does it carry closing weight: the last time the show's broadcast-era identity played out in its complete, familiar form before a full hosting replacement arrived the following year.
