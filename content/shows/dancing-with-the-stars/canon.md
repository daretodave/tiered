# Editor's Canon — Dancing with the Stars

## 1. Season 1 — The Premiere

tag: The American debut — the format proved itself in six episodes.
slot_argument: Season one established the format, the tone, and the casting frame every later season has worked inside. The six-episode run is short enough that the show never has time to lose the room — it earns the top slot on merit.
community_rank_hint: rank=1 delta=0 sentiment=hold

The first season of Dancing with the Stars ran six episodes in the summer of 2005 and settled the question of whether the format could cross the Atlantic in about forty minutes. Celebrity-professional pairings, a panel of three judges, live phone votes, weekly eliminations — the structure was borrowed from the BBC, but the casting energy was entirely its own. Production found its rhythm before the second episode and held it through a run short enough that the format's weaknesses never had time to accumulate. The canon places it first because the format it introduced has run without a structural overhaul across every season since.
