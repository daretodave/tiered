# Editor's Canon — The Real Housewives of Potomac

## 7. Season 7

tag: The most cast-stable season the show has ever run — seven housewives, zero turnover.
slot_argument: Season seven repeats Season 6's cast exactly, with no new housewife and no departure, so the group's chemistry gets to run at full maturity. It's the clearest evidence RHOP's format works best without a reset.
community_rank_hint: rank=1 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season seven earns the top spot because it's the version of RHOP that trusts its cast the most. Gizelle Bryant, Ashley Darby, Robyn Dixon, Karen Huger, Candiace Dillard Bassett, Wendy Osefo, and Mia Thornton all return unchanged from Season 6 — no new housewife, no departure, the most cast-stable stretch the show has produced. That stability lets the old-money-versus-new-money tension that defines Potomac play out between people who already know exactly how to needle each other, rather than resetting around a newcomer's introduction. Twenty episodes run with a confidence earlier seasons had to earn scene by scene. It's proof the format's specificity holds even without a fresh face to lean on.

## 4. Season 4

tag: The original six-woman core's first fully unchanged season.
slot_argument: Season four is RHOP's first taste of zero cast turnover — the same six housewives from Season 3 return with nobody new and nobody gone. It sets the template Season 7 later perfects with a bigger cast.
community_rank_hint: rank=2 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season four ranks second because it's where RHOP first proves cast stability pays off. Gizelle Bryant, Ashley Darby, Robyn Dixon, Karen Huger, Monique Samuels, and Candiace Dillard Bassett return exactly as they left Season 3, with Katie Rost popping back only as a guest. Nobody new arrives to reset the group's dynamics, and the season is stronger for it — old grievances and old alliances both carry over with real weight. Twenty-one episodes is the show's longest order to that point, and the extra runway lets a six-woman cast that's already spent two seasons circling each other finally close the distance. It's the blueprint Season 7 later runs with a bigger roster.

## 1. Season 1

tag: The founding season that gave the franchise its DC-adjacent social hierarchy.
slot_argument: Six women enter a Potomac social world defined by old money against new money — a tension no sibling franchise has filmed. It ranks behind two seasons that prove the format runs stronger once the cast has years to build on this foundation.
community_rank_hint: rank=3 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season one still earns a top-three spot because it's the version of RHOP every later season gets checked against. Karen Huger, Gizelle Bryant, Robyn Dixon, Charrisse Jackson-Jordan, Katie Rost, and Ashley Darby introduce a Black upper-middle-class Potomac world built on old money against new — a specificity no other Housewives chapter has matched. Ten episodes is a short debut by the franchise's later standards, and the founding cast, strong as it is, hasn't yet had years to accumulate the layered history that makes Seasons 4 and 7 so effective. That's the only reason it sits third rather than first: this canon rewards accumulated chemistry, and season one is where that chemistry begins rather than where it peaks.

## 3. Season 3

tag: Candiace Dillard's debut and the show's first trip abroad.
slot_argument: Season three adds a housewife who becomes a franchise mainstay and sends the cast to the French Riviera, the show's biggest structural swing yet. A two-part reunion caps a season that expands RHOP's scope in every direction.
community_rank_hint: rank=4 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season three ranks fourth on the strength of one addition and one big swing. Candiace Dillard joins as the group's sixth full-time housewife and goes on to become one of the show's longest-running cast members, while Charrisse Jackson-Jordan steps back to a recurring role after two seasons at the center. The season's real structural leap is a multi-episode trip to the French Riviera, the furthest RHOP had traveled from Potomac at that point, and the change of scenery sharpens the old-money-versus-new-money dynamic rather than diluting it. Twenty episodes and a two-part reunion give the expanded cast plenty of room. It doesn't yet have the stability of the seasons ranked above it, but the growth it introduces pays off for years.

## 6. Season 6

tag: Mia Thornton's debut and the longest reunion format yet.
slot_argument: Season six brings in a new full-time housewife after one exit, holding the cast at seven, and closes with a four-part reunion — its longest yet. Solid, but the newcomer hasn't yet built the history that makes Seasons 4 and 7 rank higher.
community_rank_hint: rank=5 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season six lands mid-canon because it's a transition season doing transition-season work well, not brilliantly. Mia Thornton joins as a new full-time housewife after Monique Samuels doesn't return, and five returning housewives — Gizelle Bryant, Ashley Darby, Robyn Dixon, Karen Huger, and Candiace Dillard Bassett, plus Wendy Osefo from the season before — give her room to find her footing. Filmed over the first half of 2021, the season closes with a four-part reunion, the longest format RHOP had used to that point, and the extended runtime pays off given how much the cast has just shifted. It's a competent, well-cast season that hasn't yet had the chance to build the kind of unbroken continuity that ranks above it.

## 5. Season 5

tag: The COVID-delayed season that grows the cast to seven.
slot_argument: Season five adds Wendy Osefo and pushes the cast to its largest size yet, but a pandemic delay and the season's late churn keep it from cracking the top half. Solid expansion, not a defining chapter.
community_rank_hint: rank=6 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season five sits just below the midpoint because expansion alone isn't enough to rank higher in this canon. Wendy Osefo joins as the group's seventh full-time housewife, growing the cast past its founding six for the first time, while a COVID-19 delay pushes the premiere from a planned spring date to August. Twenty-two episodes — the longest order yet — close with a three-part reunion, and the season also turns out to be Monique Samuels' last as a full-time housewife. The old-money-versus-new-money hierarchy is present but a little diffuse across a bigger group still finding its shape, which is why the canon holds it below the tighter, more settled seasons ranked above it.

## 2. Season 2

tag: A sophomore season interrupted by a mid-filming cast change.
slot_argument: Season two adds Monique Samuels but loses Katie Rost partway through filming, and the disruption is real — the shortest episode order of the ten and a roster that has to recalibrate mid-shoot.
community_rank_hint: rank=7 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season two ranks in the back half because its sophomore-season instability is visible on screen. Monique Samuels joins as a new full-time housewife, expanding the founding group, but Katie Rost's run ends partway through filming, and the remaining cast has to find a new footing mid-shoot rather than across a full, settled season. Fourteen episodes make it the shortest order in the show's history, which doesn't give the reshaped group much time to establish the kind of layered dynamics the higher-ranked seasons run on. The old-money-versus-new-money tension is present in flashes, but a season built around a disruption doesn't have the same room to develop it as one built on continuity.

## 8. Season 8

tag: The largest cast in RHOP history, on the eve of major turnover.
slot_argument: Season eight grows to eight full-time housewives, the biggest group yet, but the season plays in hindsight like the last stop before two of the show's longest-running cast members step away. Big roster, uncertain footing.
community_rank_hint: rank=8 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season eight sits in the canon's lower third because size alone doesn't buy stability. Nneka Ihim joins as the group's eighth full-time housewife, the largest cast RHOP has ever run, filmed over the summer of 2023. On its own terms the season holds together — nobody leaves mid-shoot the way Season 2 did — but it turns out to be Candiace Dillard Bassett's and Robyn Dixon's last as full-time housewives, two of the show's longest-running cast members, and the season plays differently once that context is visible. Ihim's run doesn't extend past this one season either. A crowded cast spreads the old-money-versus-new-money dynamic thin rather than sharpening it, which is why it ranks below the leaner, steadier seasons above it.

## 9. Season 9

tag: The heaviest cast reshuffle in show history.
slot_argument: Three housewives don't return and two new ones step in, leaving the group to rebuild its social order almost from scratch. Real churn, and the specificity that defines this canon's top tier hasn't had time to reform yet.
community_rank_hint: rank=9 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season nine ranks near the bottom because it's RHOP's most disrupted season, and the disruption shows. Candiace Dillard Bassett, Robyn Dixon, and Nneka Ihim all step away, and the show brings in Stacey Rusch as a new full-time housewife while promoting Keiarna Stewart from a recurring role — more than a third of the cast is new or newly elevated. Five returning housewives anchor a group still finding its footing rather than deepening ground it's already covered, including Mia Thornton in her final season as a full-timer. The old-money-versus-new-money hierarchy that defines this canon's best seasons needs continuity to land, and this much turnover hasn't left room to earn it.

## 10. Season 10

tag: The season the show's longest-running old-money anchor isn't there.
slot_argument: Karen Huger doesn't appear as a full-time housewife for the first time in ten seasons, a real-world absence that hits directly at the specific tension this canon rewards most. The lowest slot, for a real structural reason.
community_rank_hint: rank=10 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season ten sits last because it's missing the one element this canon has rewarded most since the debut: Karen Huger. A real-world legal matter keeps Huger off the main season for the first time in RHOP's history — she turns up only in a post-finale interview segment — and her absence lands directly on the old-money side of the show's defining tension. Tia Glover and Angel Massie join as new full-time housewives, and Mia Thornton exits after four seasons, leaving a reshuffled cast still finding its shape. That's not a knock on anyone's effort. It's simply a season built around the format's central hierarchy, missing its most identifiable old-money figure.
