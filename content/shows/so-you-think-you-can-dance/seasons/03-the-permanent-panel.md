Season three locks Nigel Lythgoe and Mary Murphy into a permanent
two-judge panel, with a rotating guest filling the third chair each
week. Auditions move through New York, Chicago, Los Angeles, and
Atlanta before ten men and ten women reach live shows, drawn into
pairs from a hat. Fixed partnerships dissolve once the field narrows
to ten, and contestants draw new partners weekly from there — a
structural shift the franchise keeps for seasons after.
