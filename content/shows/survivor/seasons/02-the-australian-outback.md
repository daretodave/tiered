Survivor's first sequel, dropped behind the Super Bowl and
filmed on a cattle station in inland Queensland. The cast plays
like people who watched Borneo and showed up ready, and the
location punishes them for it — drought, then floods, then the
heat coming back. The audience that tuned in for novelty stayed
for the texture. Outback is where Survivor stops being a stunt
and starts being a franchise.
