Subtitled Second Chance, the cast voted in by viewers from a
ballot of players who never won, shot off Koh Rong. The
construction is the hook — returnees who all want a do-over,
without veterans of the late-2000s all-star era, a roster the
audience picked. The result is a tactically dense season in the
middle of Survivor's idol-and-advantage era, paced like the show
knows the cast knows the game. A touchstone the modern returnee
seasons keep pointing back to.
