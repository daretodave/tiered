Survivor's first all-returnee season, shot in Panama and pulled
together from the first seven casts. Eighteen veterans, three
tribes, a marooning the audience didn't need explained. The
construction is the show learning what it owns — recognizable
faces it can build a season around without the slow first-act
introductions a fresh cast demands. The pacing is brisk, the
politics are loaded with seasons of prior context, and the
format begins to think of itself as a franchise with a memory.
