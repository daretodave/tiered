The season the format clicked. Twelve houseguests move in, and for the first time the houseguests do the evicting — the public-vote experiment is gone, and the show finds the engine it would run on for a quarter-century. The pacing tightens to a thrice-weekly rhythm. The cast plays harder because the stakes are now interpersonal, not televised. Julie Chen settles into her cadence. Every Big Brother season that followed is a footnote to the summer this format snapped into place.
