Season eight is the franchise running on its established infrastructure. The season-seven cast holds, Jacqueline Laurita returns, and Margaret Josephs appears in a limited capacity ahead of her full-season arrival. The problem is the absence of genuine recalibration — no new entry point, no shift in social geometry, no moment the season can build toward. Watchable by any franchise standard, but compared to what surrounds it, this is RHONJ marking time rather than generating argument.
