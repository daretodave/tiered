# Editor's Canon — Selling Sunset

## 1. Season 1

tag: The founding season — Netflix's docusoap introduces its brokerage and its format with total confidence.
slot_argument: Season one sets the format's whole grammar in eight episodes: seven agents, one brokerage, listings and office dynamics running in parallel. The cast's chemistry is immediate, and the show never pads its runtime.
community_rank_hint: { rank: 1, delta: 0, sentiment: hold }

Season one earns the top slot by default — it's the only entry so far — but it also nails the format immediately. Seven agents, one high-end brokerage, and a season structure that treats real estate and office dynamics as equally important stories. The Oppenheim Group's internal hierarchy is established fast, and the season never overexplains itself: multi-million-dollar listings function as both plot and set dressing, while the cast's confrontational chemistry surfaces early and often. Eight episodes is a tight runtime, but the show uses every one of them, and the format that nine seasons will build on is fully operational from the first episode.
