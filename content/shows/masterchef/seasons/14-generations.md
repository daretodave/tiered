MasterChef: Generations groups twenty home cooks by generation — Baby Boomers, Gen X, Millennials, Gen Z — and runs them through team challenges before the cohorts dissolve and individual competition takes over. The generational sorting produces some early culinary contrast, and a restaurant takeover challenge midseason stands out. But the generational theme exhausts its editorial value before the individual phase begins, and the back half runs without a distinguishing premise.
