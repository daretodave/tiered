Season nine is the most structurally inventive entry in the show's run. Each judge — Ramsay, Bastianich, and Sanchez — selects eight home cooks and mentors them through the full season. The mentorship model changes how the weekly challenges land: the stakes have a personal dimension that individual elimination doesn't carry. Twenty-three episodes and the largest cast in the show's history give that structure room to breathe.
