# Editor's Canon — Real Housewives of Salt Lake City

## 1. Season 1

tag: The founding season that gave the franchise its most distinct social backdrop.
slot_argument: Six women enter a Salt Lake City social world shaped by an LDS-adjacent culture unlike any sibling franchise. The cast is large for a debut, the terrain is new to Bravo, and the season sets the terms for everything RHOSLC becomes.
community_rank_hint: rank=1 delta=0 sentiment=hold

Season one introduces a six-woman founding cast, larger than most Housewives debuts, and puts them inside a social world Bravo hadn't filmed before: a Salt Lake City circle where faith, and distance from faith, quietly organizes who belongs where. That backdrop gives the show a structural identity distinct from Atlanta, Orange County, or Beverly Hills before a single confrontation plays out. The cast is confident from the opening episodes, comfortable with the cameras and with each other's company, and the setting itself — mountain-ringed, image-conscious, a little insular — does real editorial work rather than sitting as scenery. It's an unusually assured debut, and the only season ranked here so far, which makes its founding-slot placement straightforward rather than contested.
